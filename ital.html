Project background

A UX design team at Pratt Institute is designing a digital product for meal prepping . Working in a team of two, we designed and prototyped an Afro-Caribbean Vegan recipe application. From January to May 2020, I established the user research, design layout, and comparative analysis. Mainly designed for vegans, I wanted the design to be educational towards Afro Caribbean culture. Our team decided our user group would include vegans, and people transitioning to vegan. This project was completed during the peak of COVID-19.

Yummly is a mobile app that assists individuals in finding new recipes and provides detailed instructions on how to make these cuisines. The app recommends recipes based on personalized user tastes, recipes searches, and much more. Also available on both android and iOS, it is also praised for it attractive interface and graphics.


Takeaways
Personalization: the app uses social media integration to offer personalized recommendations. Ex: Google, Facebook,& Email. It also allows users to “um” recipes they like to also personalize recommendations.
Recipes: recipes are provided and organized into three sections: Just for you, Explore, and Store.
User Interface: provides a visual of the recipe in a Material Design-inspired animation along with instructions and nutrition facts in a slick animation.



Tasty, which is also an online publication, is a smartphone app which houses unlimited amounts of recipe tutorials along side-by-side visuals and instructions. Available on the both iOS and android, the app allows users to filter their recipe selections by ingredients, cuisine, and social occasion while also allowing personalization to dietary restrictions.

Takeaways
Personalization: once starting the app, users can indicate food preferences such as vegetarian, gluten free, etc.
Recipes: includes a section of special cloud stickers to increase recipe discoverability. It also allows creating a favorite recipes sections.
Step by Step Instructions: recipe tutorials are available in full-screen videos or written instructions, depending on user’s preference.
